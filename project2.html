Gocation

Gocation is an Android app that uses low energy bluetooth technology to trace and track the whereabouts of festival or large event attendees. It works by monitoring the movements of users with beacons then sending notifications to other users of their current location. Beacons can be placed at all major points of interest around the event/s. For example, at a festival there will be beacons placed at the main entrance, entrances to all stages, toilets, bar and food entrances etc. The issue was discovered from personal experiences of losing friends at large events. The target audience for this project is people between the ages of 18 and 30 who attend large events and festivals.

Backend of the app

Facebook Auth Login
Firebase Database
When a user logs in to the app, they login through their Facebook account and accept the permissions. The permissions are access to:
- Public Profile (Profile Image etc.)
- Full Name
- Email Address
- Gender
The Facebook data is then stored in Firebase under the userid child There is also a lastSeenAt child which is where last beacon detection of that particular user is stored.

Messaging

Our app also has a realtime inbuilt messaging app, utilising the features of Firebase. Everyone who has joined the app is able to send a group message to everyone else within the app. Everyone else will also get a push notification when this is complete.

Beacons and Notifications

The main feature of our app is beacon triggered push notifications. When one user passes an identified beacon, every other user in the firebase gets a push notification of their activity. The push notification is not given to the user that is passing the beacon. The notifications are triggered by changes in the firebase data and are updated on the main screen underneath the userâ€™s name.



SOS feature

As an extra feature, we have also added an SOS swipe feature. It works by sending a push notification to everyone else in the database when it is swiped. The idea behind this feature was if people were lost or in trouble, they could trigger the SOS switch to send an urgent notification to their friends via the chat feature. The swipe function is situated on the user profile so it is easily accessible in an emergency. The swipe action is better then a button because it is much less likely to be accidently activated.
